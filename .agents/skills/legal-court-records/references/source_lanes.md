# Legal And Court Source Lanes

Use official court sources first, then public mirrors and strong legal reporting.

## Official Court Records

- Court-hosted docket pages and case search portals.
- Electronic filing systems such as CM/ECF, PACER, state e-filing portals, bankruptcy courts, appellate dockets, and agency adjudication portals.
- Orders, opinions, judgments, verdict forms, sentencing entries, plea documents, notices, motions, briefs, exhibits, and transcripts when public.
- Bankruptcy petitions, schedules, first-day declarations, plans, confirmation orders, sale orders, examiner/trustee reports, and claims registers where public.

## Public Mirrors And Archives

- CourtListener and RECAP for public PACER mirrors; verify docket/document metadata when possible.
- Official state archive pages, court press pages, regulator enforcement pages, and appellate opinion repositories.
- Internet Archive or other web archives for disappeared court pages; note capture date and original URL.

## Secondary Sources

- Reputable legal journalism, local reporting, investigative reporting, appellate coverage, and scholarly/legal analysis.
- Law firm blogs or party press releases are self-interested context unless they quote or link the underlying record.
- Aggregators, docket summaries, and scraped databases are leads unless the underlying filing or order is visible.

## Source Grading Defaults

- `A`: official docket, filing, order, opinion, judgment, transcript, public exhibit, bankruptcy document, or regulator adjudication record.
- `B`: reputable legal/local/investigative reporting with named records or direct links.
- `C`: party statement, law firm/client alert, self-published case page, documentary/interview about the case.
- `D`: scraped docket summary, uncited blog/forum/social post, commercial database row without underlying record.
- `X`: AI summaries, fabricated/provenance-free content, sealed/private material without permission, or unlawfully obtained material.
