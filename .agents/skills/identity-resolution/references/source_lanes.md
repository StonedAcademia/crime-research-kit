# Identity Resolution Source Lanes

Use public sources that connect a name or alias to a role, date, place, organization, or document. Avoid private identifiers.

## Strong Identity Anchors

- Court filings, dockets, opinions, and public exhibits that name a role or party.
- Corporate, nonprofit, regulator, licensing, or public-office records that name a person or organization in a public role.
- Official biographies, institution pages, public alumni/faculty/staff profiles, archived official pages, and public reports.
- Signed articles, books, interviews, transcripts, conference programs, public testimony, and filings with dates.
- Multiple independent sources that align on name, role, time period, and jurisdiction.

## Conflict And Exclusion Lanes

- Records showing incompatible dates, jurisdictions, roles, ages, organizations, or known aliases.
- Corrections, clarifications, court findings, or later reporting that distinguishes people with the same name.
- Source notes that one profile or filing was misattributed.

## Weak Or Lead-Only Sources

- People-search sites, scraped directories, commercial aggregators, social media, forums, wikis, and AI summaries.
- Address, phone, email, family-member, precise school, employer, medical, or financial details that are private-person information.

## Source Grading Defaults

- `A`: official public record or official profile directly tying the identity to a public role.
- `B`: reputable reporting, transcript, book, or archive with named sourcing and clear identity context.
- `C`: self-report, memoir, interview, personal site, profile, or institutional page that may be stale.
- `D`: aggregator, social post, forum, scraped directory, uncited wiki, or same-name lead.
- `X`: AI summary, unverifiable content, private/doxxing material, or fabricated/provenance-free source.
