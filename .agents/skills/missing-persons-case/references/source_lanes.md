# Missing-Person Source Lanes

Use public-interest sources only. Register each source before extraction.

## Primary/Public Official

- Law-enforcement missing-person bulletins and press releases.
- Public missing-person database entries.
- Public unidentified-person database entries when a comparison is warranted.
- Coroner, medical examiner, or court records only when public and directly relevant.
- Agency updates that mark a person located, recovered, identified, or misidentified.

## Strong Secondary

- Local reporting closest to the disappearance, recovery, or status update.
- Later investigative reporting that cites official records or named sources.
- Reputable documentary, transcript, or archive sources with clear provenance.

## Lead-Only

- Public social-media posts from official agencies or verified organizations.
- Family-organized public pages only as pointers to official/reporting sources.
- Forums, wikis, summaries, and unsourced timelines.

Lead-only sources can guide searches but cannot establish status, identity, foul play, or case relevance.

## Search Patterns

Use combinations of:

- Name and aliases with `missing`, `last seen`, `reported missing`, `located`, `recovered`, `unidentified`, `Doe`, `case number`, `bulletin`, `law enforcement`, and jurisdiction.
- Broad place plus date range and event terms.
- Vehicle, organization, school/institution, route, or public event name when source-stated.
- Status terms such as `located safely`, `remains found`, `identified`, `misidentified`, `correction`, and `retraction`.

## Privacy Defaults

- Exact home, school, shelter, workplace, treatment facility, or minor-related locations default to non-public.
- Tip-line and agency public-contact details may be cited as source metadata but should not be amplified in public exports unless necessary.
- Non-public relatives, witnesses, classmates, coworkers, and neighbors stay out of public exports.
