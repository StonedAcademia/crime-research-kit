# Claim Contradiction Source Lanes

Use these lanes to find sources that correct, retract, deny, narrow, or complicate existing claims.

## Strong Contradiction Sources

- Court findings, orders, judgments, appellate decisions, acquittals, dismissals, reversals, amended filings, and official corrections.
- Publisher corrections, retractions, editor notes, revised articles, archival update notices, and official errata.
- Later official reports, regulator findings, agency records, coroner/medical examiner records where lawful and public, and public transcripts.
- Firsthand interviews or testimony that directly denies, narrows, or qualifies an earlier claim.
- Strong investigative follow-ups that compare records and name sources.

## Context And Caveat Sources

- Source-independence evidence showing wire-copy repetition, press-release repetition, or same-source chains.
- Expert context that explains why a claim is disputed without itself resolving the fact.
- Timeline sources that show date, place, or sequence conflicts.

## Weak Or Lead-Only Sources

- Social posts, forums, uncited blogs, AI summaries, search snippets, repeated rumors, or commentary that does not cite source records.
- Anonymous claims without corroborating public records.
- Private/doxxing material, sealed records, minor/private-person details, or unlawfully obtained content.

## Source Grading Defaults

- `A`: official correction, court finding/order, public official report, amended filing, transcript, or primary record.
- `B`: reputable reporting or documentary source with direct evidence and named sourcing.
- `C`: firsthand interview, memoir, self-report, party statement, or expert context that needs corroboration.
- `D`: lead-only commentary, social/forum/wiki/aggregator material, repeated rumor, or same-source repetition.
- `X`: AI summary, fabricated/provenance-free content, private material without permission, or unlawful source.
