# Criminal Research Forensic Lenses

Use these lenses as organizing concepts. They do not replace evidence.

## Core Distinctions

- **M.O.**: functional, source-observed behavior used to carry out an offense. It can change.
- **Signature-like behavior**: expressive or repetitive behavior a source or expert distinguishes from functional necessity. Treat as hypothesis unless sourced.
- **Motive**: stated or adjudicated reason. Do not infer it from behavior alone.
- **Victimology**: source-backed context about victims and selection patterns. Avoid blame and private details.
- **Offense script**: sequence of approach, control, offense conduct, exit, concealment, or post-offense behavior at a high level.
- **Escalation/de-escalation**: change over time supported by multiple dated events or an expert source.

## Source Hierarchy

Prefer peer-reviewed criminology/forensic psychology, court findings, official reports, and primary transcripts. Use investigative journalism and documentaries as secondary sources. Treat profiler commentary, memoirs, social posts, forum claims, and unattributed summaries as leads unless corroborated.

## Safe Analysis Pattern

1. State the source-backed observation.
2. Identify the forensic lens.
3. Add confidence and caveats.
4. List competing explanations or missing records.
5. Mark public export false when the point is diagnostic, private, weakly sourced, or likely to overstate culpability.
