# Property And Location Source Lanes

Use official property and map sources first. Treat exact addresses as sensitive even when public.

## Official Records

- County/city assessor records, parcel lookup, GIS maps, tax rolls, and property cards.
- Recorder/register of deeds: deeds, mortgages, liens, easements, transfers, book/page, instrument numbers.
- Permit, building, zoning, planning, inspection, fire, health, and code-enforcement records.
- Court, bankruptcy, foreclosure, condemnation, eminent-domain, probate, or receivership records when public.
- Public institution/campus/facility maps, archival maps, and government reports.

## Secondary Sources

- Reputable reporting, historical archives, institutional histories, public maps, aerial imagery, and corporate filings.
- Real-estate listing pages, people-search sites, and commercial data brokers are lead-only unless they link to official records.

## Source Grading Defaults

- `A`: official assessor/recorder/permit/GIS/court/government record.
- `B`: reputable reporting, institutional archive, official map, or corporate filing that identifies the property/facility.
- `C`: self-published organizational page, real-estate listing, historical narrative, or map needing corroboration.
- `D`: data broker, scraped listing, people-search page, forum/social post, or uncited map.
- `X`: AI summary, private/doxxing material, fabricated/provenance-free content, or unlawful source.
